Welcome to Connections

Here you can learn about cultures similar to Māori. This can be with language, cooking food, and transportation.

Enjoy your experience using the Earth model to the right by selecting a country to learn more. To use the earth, just click and drag to rotate the earth around and pick a marker on the earth.

New Zealand

New Zealand is home to over 120 different Iwi and Hapu across the country. These Iwi have their own traditions, from how they act, speak, and express themselves. From these Iwi, you can find the history of generations of Māori across the world, as well as Māori of today.

Japan

Japan shares a very similar way of speaking to Māori Culture. This is seen with Japanese having similar pronunciation and even grammar. This is seen with Japanese saying their sentences backwards, like Māori. This could be a result of Japanese and Māori being connected to each other, before Māori were in Hawaiki.

Mexico

Similar to Māori, Mexican culture shares a similarity with cooking food in the ground. This is seen in their dish called Barbacoa, where they use 'Earth Ovens' to cook their, similar to a Māori Hangi. They also share a similarity of using leaves when cooking the food to change the way that the food is cooked.

Scotland

In Scottish origins, Vikings would travel the sea using Longships. These Longships required oars and sails to move through the water, similar to a Waka used by Māori. These boats were made to carry large amounts of supplies across the seas, each with their subtle differences between tribes / iwi.

China

In Chinese Culture, the Lunar New Year is celebrated to mark the beginning of the lunar calendar. This calendar uses the position of the moon, and can have different characteristics depending on the time of year. This is similar to Māori's Maramataka, which is also a lunar calendar that the Māori use to track seasons and spiritual wellbeing.
