Welcome to Connections

Here you can learn about other cultures that have similar traditions and cultures to Maori, from similar language styles, methods to cook food, and transportation.

Enjoy your experience using the globe model to the right to select a country and learn more about their connection to Maori Culture

New Zealand

New Zealand is home to over 120 different Iwi and Hapu across the country, each with their own traditions and culture variation. These Iwi each have their own differences in how they act, speak, and express themselves to others and are the foundations of generations of Maori across New Zealand.

Japan

Japan shares a very similar way of speaking to Maori Culture, with Japanese having similar pronounciation and even grammar. This is because like Maori, Japanese also speak backwards, with their order of ideas reverse to English.

Mexico

Much like a traditional Maori meal called a Hangi, Mexican culture shares a similar tradition of cooking food by burying it in the ground using an 'Earth Oven'. This is seen in their dish called Barbacoa and can also use leaves to change the flavour of the food, much like a Hangi.

Scotland

In Scottish origins, Vikings would traverse the sea using Longships that required oars and sails to move through the water. This is similar to the Maori Waka, that were used to cross the distance from Hawaiki to New Zealand, both with the ability to carry tribes as well as supplies.

China

Each year, Chinese cultures celebrate their Lunar New Year, using the position of the moon to guide the lunar calendar throughout the year. This is also seen with Maori Culture as they use the Maramataka as a calendar to guide them through harvesting seasons and spiritual wellbeing.
